§ 1 — Overview

LMS Scope & Metrics

Once a client is created and posted through the LOS, the account transitions to the Loan Management Service (LMS). LMS governs everything during the active life of the loan.

§ 1.1 Product Types

Loans Against Mutual Funds (LAMF)

Loans that allow mutual funds to be pledged as collateral for a loan.

Loans Against Securities (LAS)

Loans that allow securities such as Shares, Bonds, and Equity to be pledged as collateral for a loan.

§ 1.2 Loan Types

OD Loan (Overdraft)

Standard revolving credit facility offered to all customers. Customer draws and repays freely within the Drawing Power limit.

Term Loan

Fixed-schedule loan product. Offered exclusively by CRED as the loan service provider. Not available on the standard Volt platform offering.

§ 1.3 Key Financial Metrics

POS
Principal Outstanding
The live outstanding principal balance on the loan at any point in time.
DP
Drawing Power
Maximum amount the customer is permitted to draw.
Units × NAV × LTV
TOS
Total Outstanding
Sum of all outstanding amounts.
POS + Interest + Charges
LTV
Loan to Value
Ratio applied per fund category to determine how much of the collateral's market value can be lent. Typically 45%; higher for Equity and Debt funds.
NAV
Net Asset Value
Per-unit market price of a mutual fund. Updated daily at 4:00 AM.
AL
Available Limit
Funds the customer can withdraw at current DP.
DP − TOS
SL
Sanctioned Limit
Total funds the customer can theoretically borrow. Set at ₹2 Cr or ₹5 Cr depending on need.

§ 1.3 Daily Processing Schedule

4:00 AM

NAV Update

Latest NAV values ingested for all pledged fund units.

6:30 AM

DP Recalculation

Drawing Power recomputed using updated NAV × Units × LTV.

6:30 AM

Shortfall Check

If POS > DP after recalculation, the account enters shortfall status.

§ 2 — Collateral Management

Pledging, Adding & Removing

Collaterals are pledged during the LOS journey but are not automatically added to the loan account. The Add Collateral step in LMS formally associates pledged units with the loan and makes them eligible for DP calculation.

➕

§ 2.1 Add Collateral

Customer's holding statement is fetched. Selected mutual fund units are posted to LMS. On successful posting, DP is computed and the loan becomes eligible for disbursement up to the computed DP.

💡
DP enhancement: a customer with an existing loan can add further collateral at any time, subject to applicable processing fees.
➖

§ 2.2 Remove Collateral

A customer may request removal of collateral from the loan account, subject to a critical constraint:

⚠️
Post-removal DP must remain ≥ POS. The customer cannot remove collateral that would cause POS to exceed new Drawing Power. On approval, units are unlodged but remain pledged.
🔴

§ 2.3 Sell-off Collateral

When collateral value reduces due to market fluctuations, DP reduces. If DP falls below POS, account enters shortfall. If unresolved within 7 days, collateral is liquidated. Normally 2× the shortfall value is sold to prevent further shortfall.

§ 2.3 DP Calculation Formula

DP = Σ ( Units × NAV × LTV ) for each collateral folio
NAV refreshed daily at 4:00 AM  |  Shortfall check at 6:30 AM  |  Default LTV: 45% (higher for Equity & Debt funds)
MinLTV and MaxLTV defined in script master  |  LSP-specific overrides via overrideLTV field
§ 3 — Fund Disbursement

Customer-Initiated Drawdown

Disbursement is a customer-initiated action. The customer requests a drawdown of funds up to their Available Limit (DP minus existing POS).

§ 3.1 Disbursement Flow

#StageDescriptionActor
1 Customer Request Customer requests a specific draw amount within the available limit. Customer
2 Eligibility Check Platform validates: requested amount + POS ≤ DP. Volt
3 Fee Deduction Applicable platform or processing fees deducted. Disbursement amount = requested amount + ₹1 (statutory minimum over fees). Volt
4 Fund Transfer Funds moved from DSP Finance's account to the customer's registered bank account via Yes Bank (sponsor bank). Yes Bank
5 Third-Party Processing End-to-end disbursement orchestrated by Cashfree (third-party payment partner). Cashfree
6 POS Update POS is incremented by the disbursed principal amount. Transactions and charges are posted. LMS

§ 3.2 Disbursement Parties

ℹ️
The disbursement amount is the requested principal plus ₹1, which covers the minimum statutory transfer amount over any applicable platform or processing fees.
§ 4 — Interest Accrual & Payment

Simple Interest, Monthly Due

§ 4.1 Accrual Rules

→

Interest is calculated only on the Principal Outstanding (POS), not on the full DP.

→

Calculation method: Simple Interest, applied daily at the contractual interest rate.

→

Accrued interest accumulates and is due at the end of each calendar month.

→

Principal repayment is entirely optional at any time. Only interest is mandatory on a monthly basis.

Daily Interest = POS × ( Annual Rate ÷ 365 )

§ 4.2 Interest Payment

📅

Frequency

Monthly, due at mandate day of month.

🔗

Collection Method

Mandate acquired from the customer during LOS (DIGIO). Auto-debited via Razorpay.

💳

Payment Provider

Razorpay handles all repayment collections — including interest and principal.

§ 5 — Repayments

Waterfall & Virtual Accounts

Repayments are accepted at any time and processed through Razorpay. All repayments follow a strict waterfall priority order.

§ 5.1 Repayment Waterfall

1
Interest Outstanding
All accrued but unpaid interest is cleared first.
2
Charges
Any fees, penalties, or processing charges are settled next.
3
Principal (POS)
Any remaining repayment amount is applied to reduce the Principal Outstanding.

§ 5.2 Repayment Types

Interest Repayment

Mandatory, monthly, auto-collected via mandate through Razorpay.

MANDATORY

Principal Repayment

Fully voluntary; customers can repay any amount at any time to reduce POS and free up DP headroom.

OPTIONAL

§ 5.3 Virtual Accounts

  • → Each loan account gets an attached virtual account to which payments can be made if normal financial routes are not available.
  • → Repayments can only be made by blood relatives such as Father, Mother, Siblings, etc.
  • → Any funds transferred to this account are counted as payment towards the loan account.
  • → The last 6 digits of the VA are the last 6 digits of FXLAN.
§ 6 — Shortfall Handling

NAV Decline & Liquidation

A shortfall occurs when POS exceeds Drawing Power, typically caused by a fall in NAV of pledged mutual fund units. Detected during the daily 6:30 AM DP recalculation.

§ 6.1 Shortfall Flow

#StageDescriptionActor
1 Shortfall Detected Daily 6:30 AM check: POS > DP after NAV update. Account flagged as in shortfall. System
2 Customer Notification Customer notified of shortfall amount (POS − DP) and given 6 days to resolve it. System
3 Repay Balance Customer may repay the shortfall amount within the 6-day window to bring POS back within DP. Customer
4 Day 7 — No Action If no sufficient repayment by end of day 6, automated sell-off is triggered on day 7. System
5 Sell-Off Collateral Mutual fund units equivalent in value to 2× the shortfall amount are liquidated to service the loan and restore DP headroom. Volt
⚡
Sell-off is calibrated to 2× the shortfall amount — not just the shortfall itself — to create a buffer against further NAV decline and against DP reducing due to the sell-off itself. Post sell-off, POS is reduced and DP is recalculated.

§ 6.3 Shortfall States

Normal
POS ≤ DP
Customers can draw funds freely up to the available limit.
Shortfall — Grace Period
POS > DP
Customers have up to 6 days to repay the excess. New disbursements blocked.
Sell-Off Triggered
Day 7, unresolved
System automatically liquidates 2× shortfall in collateral units.
§ 7 — Loan Closure

Conditions & Steps

A loan can be closed when both conditions are simultaneously satisfied.

Condition 1
TOS = 0

All principal + interest + charges fully repaid. TOS = POS + Accrued Interest + Any Pending Charges.

Condition 2
Tenure = 36 months

Maximum tenure reached (36 months or as defined by LSP). The customer can request tenure extension.

§ 7.1 Closure Steps

1
Customer repays all outstanding interest and charges.
2
The customer repays the full principal (POS reduced to zero).
3
TOS reaches zero — system flags account as eligible for closure.
4
Tenure condition verified (36 months elapsed).
5
All pledged collateral units are unpledged and returned to the customer's folio.
6
The loan account is marked closed in Finflux (LMS core).

§ 9 — Third-Party Integrations (LMS)

External Providers

Cashfree
Disbursement Orchestration
End-to-end payment processing for fund disbursements to customer bank accounts.
Yes Bank
Sponsor Bank
Regulated intermediary that executes the physical fund transfer from DSP Finance to the customer.
Razorpay
Repayment Collection
Processes all incoming repayments (interest, charges, principal) using the mandate collected during LOS.
Finflux
Core LMS Platform
Stores loan account state, POS, DP, transaction history, and handles account closure.
DIGIO
Mandate Provider (via LOS)
Mandate registered during LOS is used by Razorpay for auto-debit of monthly interest.
DSP Finance
Capital Source
Holds the capital pool from which loan amounts are disbursed. Funding source for all drawdowns.
