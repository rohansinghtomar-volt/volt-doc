§ 1 — Overview

What is LAMF & LAS?

The Loan Against Mutual Funds (LAMF) and Loan Against Securities (LAS) products let customers pledge existing holdings as collateral in exchange for a revolving credit line. The LOS covers the full lifecycle from lead capture to loan account creation. Loan Management (LMS) is documented separately.

System Actors

Customer — End borrower who applies for the loan
Partner — Affiliate / referral entity directing customers to Volt Money
DSP Finance — Money lender and finance provider
Volt Money Platform — End-to-end API orchestrator, acts as LSP
LSP — Loan service provider presenting opportunity to DSP Finance

§ 2 — High-Level Origination Flow

Lead Creation → Disbursement

Every major stage from lead creation to loan disbursement, in sequence.

01
Lead Creation
PAN + sourcing channel + product submitted. A Lead record is created.
02
PAN Fetch
Customer enters PAN. Details fetched via Dcentro (migrating to NSDL).
03
Phone / OTP
Customer enters mobile number. OTP dispatched via MFC to retrieve all linked mutual fund folios.
04
Opportunity Creation
Lead enriched with utilities (mobile, email, PAN, BV, address, pledging, agreement, KFS, mandate). BRE runs credit approval. Opportunity moves to approved state.
05
KYC
Full KYC performed through DIGIO (Digilocker, selfie, KYC summary, additional details).
06
Bank Verification
Bank account verified via penny drop.
07
Mandate Setup
Mandate registered through DIGIO. See §5 for mandate types.
08
Pledging & Agreement
Mutual fund units pledged as collateral. KFS and loan agreement presented and accepted.
09
Credit Approval
Final step of the LOS pipeline.
10
Client & Loan Creation
Client entity created via /create-customer API. Loan account created and lodged in Finflux (LMS).

§ 3.2 LSP-Specific Rules

Maximum loan tenure is fixed at 3 years as set by the Lender (DSP Finance). LSPs can set their own tenure within this limit.

LSP creates the lead; the customer completes the opportunity journey on the loan offer page.

LSPs can perform the flow in a different order subject to limitations. For example, some LSPs perform PAN fetch after KYC — but mandate setup cannot be done before KYC.

The high-level flow in §2 represents the journey on the Volt platform specifically.

§ 4 — KYC Process

Know Your Customer

KYC is performed through DIGIO, Volt's KYC and mandate service provider. The journey covers four components.

🏛️

Digilocker

Pulls identity documents (Aadhaar, PAN card) directly from government repositories.

🏦

Additional Details

Collects and validates supplementary bank account information for disbursement.

🤳

Selfie KYC

Liveness check via camera to confirm the applicant matches their ID documents.

📋

KYC Summary

Consolidated view of all KYC data collected, presented to the customer for confirmation.

⚠️
If there are any discrepancies or deviations in KYC and customer-provided details, the application must be approved by support in the command center. Beyond a set deviation limit, the application is REJECTED.
§ 5 — Mandate Setup

Mandate Registration

All mandates are registered through DIGIO. There are four mandate types available depending on the customer's preference. Mandates are used for automatic interest payments in the LMS and are compulsory for every loan account.

API Mandate
Debit / Credit / Aadhaar
Programmatic registration via DIGIO APIs. Supports debit, credit, or Aadhaar-based authentication.
UPI Mandate
UPI Handle
Customers provide their UPI handle; used for payment collections through the UPI ecosystem.
eSign Mandate
Aadhaar → Bank Account
Aadhaar-based electronic signature linking the mandate to the customer's bank account.
Physical Mandate
Paper Form
Customer completes and submits a physical form; used as a fallback when digital methods are unavailable.
§ 6 — Collateral Pledging

Pledging & Key Terms

Mutual fund units and securities are pledged as collateral before the loan account is created.

§ 6.2 Key Financial Terms

POS
Principal Outstanding
The outstanding principal amount on the loan at any point in time.
DP
Drawing Power
Maximum amount the customer can draw against the pledged collateral; recalculated daily.
TOS
Total Outstanding
Total outstanding funds to be paid by the customer — includes POS and any fees / interest accrued.
SL
Sanctioned Limit
Total funds the customer can theoretically borrow. Set at ₹2 Cr or ₹5 Cr depending on need.
AL
Available Limit
Funds available for withdrawal at the current DP. Calculated as DP − TOS.

§ 6.3 KFS & Loan Agreement

📄

Key Fact Statement (KFS)

A standardised summary of loan terms, fees, and charges mandated by the regulator. Must be reviewed and accepted before loan sanction.

📝

Loan Agreement

The binding contract between the customer and Volt Money governing the credit facility. Must be accepted before loan sanction.

§ 7 — Loan Account Creation

LOS → LMS Handoff

Once all LOS steps are complete, the client and loan account are created and handed off to the Loan Management Service.

§ 7.1 Credit Approval (BRE)

Credit Approval is the final step of the LOS pipeline. The Business Rule Engine (BRE) runs automated credit decisioning based on:

Risk Profile

Customer risk profile and KYC data evaluated against credit policy.

Collateral Quality

Fund category, applicable haircuts, and quality of pledged securities.

Regulatory Parameters

Regulatory and internal credit policy parameters applied automatically.

On approval, the opportunity status transitions to:

✓ APPROVED_NOT_DISBURSED

The loan account becomes active in LMS.

§ 7.2 Client Creation

POST /create-customer

Called to register the customer entity. This is the first LMS entity created and marks the formal transition from origination to servicing.

→ Customer profile persisted with KYC data, bank details, and mandate references.
→ A client record is lodged in Finflux (the core LMS platform).

§ 9 — User Types & Partner Model

Roles & Affiliate Structure

§ 9.1 User Classifications

🏢

Partner

An affiliate or business entity that refers customers to Volt Money via the affiliate system.

👤

Borrower

Any user who has an active or in-progress loan application.

ℹ️
A user can be both a partner and a borrower at the same time.

§ 9.2 Partner Affiliate Model

Partners direct customers to Volt Money and are tracked through a sourcing channel attribute on the lead record. The origination flow is identical for partner-referred customers, but the lead carries the partner's sourcing channel identifier, enabling attribution and commission tracking.
